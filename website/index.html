Job Grouping & Data Access

A structured, rule-based framework that maps every job title to a permission level — ensuring the right people see the right data, automatically.

Why This Framework Matters

Data access decisions today are often made case-by-case. This framework makes them automatic, auditable, and consistent.

📊

One Definition, Everywhere

Right now, "Teacher" means something different depending on who built the dashboard. One report includes paraprofessionals; another counts TIRs; another doesn't. This framework gives every report, dashboard, and analysis the same definitions — automatically, because they all pull from the same grouping layer.

  • Headcount reports that agree across teams
  • Turnover trends by consistent job group
  • Compensation benchmarking by standardised tier
  • Filters that mean the same thing in every tool
🔐

Principled Data Access

Instead of granting permissions individually — and inconsistently — every person inherits access rules directly from their job grouping. No more one-off tickets or forgotten over-permissioned accounts.

  • Permission changes flow from job changes
  • Off-boarding removes access automatically
  • Audit trail tied to job records
🔄

Scales with the Org

When new job titles are created, the system alerts administrators instead of silently granting default (or wrong) access. The mapping CSV is the single source of truth — easy to review and update.

  • Slack alert for any new unrecognised title
  • Admin review queue before access is granted
  • No code changes needed to update groupings
🔒

Access is Default Deny

Every person starts with zero access. The only data they can see is what their job grouping explicitly grants — nothing more. This means there are no accidental over-permissions, no silent inheritance from old roles, and no guesswork about who can see what. If a permission isn't listed for your level, you don't have it.

Row-level visibility is also downward-only by default: you can only see records for people at a lower numeric level than your own. You cannot see your peers, your managers, or anyone at or above your level unless a Peers or Above grant has been explicitly issued — and currently, no one holds this designation.

⛔ No access by default
✅ Explicit grants only
👇 Downward visibility only
🔁 Revoked on role change
📋 Every grant is auditable
🔑 Peers or Above: not currently issued

The 7 Grouping Levels

Every job title is assigned to one of seven levels. Each level inherits a defined set of data access permissions — no configuration needed per person.

Shared Definitions Across Every Report

Today, the same word can mean different things in different dashboards. This framework eliminates that ambiguity by tying every reporting filter directly to a grouping. When all tools pull from the same definitions, cross-team analyses finally add up.

Before Inconsistent — depends on who built it
"Teacher"
Might include lead teachers only, or also TIRs, paraprofessionals, subs, SPED teachers, instructional aides — depending on the report author's interpretation.
"School Leadership"
Could mean just the School Leader, or all Level 4–5 staff, or anyone with "Leader" in their title. Headcount numbers don't match across teams.
"Ops Staff"
No agreed boundary. Some reports include custodians; others start at SOM; others include central Operations staff.
"Instructional Staff"
Sometimes teachers only, sometimes teachers + coaches + ASLs, sometimes anyone who touches instruction.
"KTAF Staff"
Manually curated lists that go stale. No single agreed definition; different teams maintain their own copies.
→
After Consistent — same answer in every tool
"Teacher"
Exactly Teacher (6) — lead teachers on the lead teacher salary scale. TIRs are Teacher in Residence (6), paras are School-based Non-Instructional (6). No overlap, no ambiguity.
"School Leadership"
Level 4 School Leader grouping = the named school leader or School Leader in Residence. ASLs are their own Level 5 group. Every report uses the same boundary.
"Ops Staff"
School-facing ops = ADSOs, Ops-SOMs, AOMs & Receptionists (6). Central = KTAF or Regional Staff (5) in Operations dept. Clearly separated, never conflated.
"Instructional Staff"
Defined as Level 6 groupings: Teacher (6) + Teacher in Residence (6). Filters in every dashboard resolve to the same set of people.
"KTAF Staff"
Automatically derived: all staff in KTAF or Regional Staff (5) and above in central departments. No manual lists to maintain.

Special Access Designations

Some roles require data access that goes beyond what their job grouping level provides. These are granted as named team designations — applied to individuals in addition to their standard level permissions.

👤

CEO

The Chief Executive Officer receives unrestricted access to all data across all locations, departments, and data types — including salary, PM, and survey responses.

Applies to: Chief Executive Officer
📊

Data Team

Members of the Data team require access to all data to build and maintain dashboards, pipelines, and reports. This designation grants full read access across all data types.

Applies to: Data department staff
🧑‍💼

HR Team

Human Resources staff need access to all staff records — including sensitive employment data — regardless of location or department. Does not extend to student data.

Applies to: Human Resources department staff
💰

Finance, Accounting & Compliance Team

Finance, Accounting, and Compliance staff require full data access to support budgeting, auditing, and regulatory reporting across the organisation.

Applies to: Finance, Accounting, and Compliance department staff
Rarely Granted
🔑

Peers or Above

By default, every person can only see data for staff at a lower numeric level than their own — never peers, never senior staff. The Peers or Above designation removes this restriction: the holder can see records for people at any level, including their own and above.

This is one of the most powerful permissions in the system. It is not granted by job grouping — it must be explicitly assigned to an individual and requires a clear, documented justification. Currently, no staff member holds this designation. If ever issued, it would require explicit approval and a logged reason in the audit trail.

One-Off & Temporary Access Requests

Standard job-grouping permissions cover the vast majority of access needs. When someone needs access that goes beyond their level — for a project, an audit, or a time-limited initiative — it can be granted explicitly, with a built-in expiration date and a full audit trail.

1

Request

Requester submits an access request specifying the data scope needed, the business reason, and a proposed expiration date.

→
2

Approve

An authorised approver (typically the relevant department head or data steward) reviews and approves or denies the request.

→
3

Grant

Access is granted with a hard expiration date. The grant is logged with timestamp, approver, scope, and reason.

→
4

Expire

Access is automatically revoked at the cut-off date. No manual follow-up required — and no risk of forgotten permissions.

📅
Hard expiration dates

Every temporary grant must have a cut-off date. Access cannot be granted indefinitely through this mechanism.

🔍
Full audit trail

Every grant is logged with who requested it, who approved it, the scope, the reason, and timestamps for both grant and expiry.

🔔
Expiry notifications

Automated reminders are sent before access expires, so requesters can re-apply if the need is ongoing — rather than access silently lapsing mid-project.

⬆️
Recurring need → role review

If a temporary grant is renewed repeatedly, that's a signal the person's job grouping may need to be revisited — not that temporary access should become permanent.

How the System Works

Three files and a simple Python classifier power the entire framework.

1

Source of Truth (SOT)

allowed_jobs_sot.csv — the canonical list of every allowed job title and department combination in the organisation.

→
2

Classifier

A rule-based Python algorithm maps each (job title, department) pair to a grouping using priority-ordered pattern matching. Ambiguous cases are flagged for admin review.

→
3

Mapping CSV

job_groupings_mapping.csv — the editable output. Admins can override any auto-generated grouping here. This is the source of truth for runtime lookups.

→
4

New Job Alert

Any job title not in the mapping triggers a Slack alert. An admin reviews it and adds it to the mapping before access is granted — no silent permission escalation.
